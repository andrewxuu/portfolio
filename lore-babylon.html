The Realm

Silva Aetherea

For millennia this land shaped and sculpted itself, with water carving valleys and riverways and the ground splitting and colliding into rocky mountains and stony hills. Trees and plants of every kind took root in the fertile soil until lush forest covered the landscape in a thick blanket of green. Its trees have watched people and animals rise and fall, and many who wandered into its vast expanse were never found again.

An early civilization gave the land its name: Silva Aetherea, the "Ethereal Forest." They revered it as sacred, a mother that sustained countless lives. To take from the forest without giving back was taboo: harvested plants had their seeds replanted, and no part of a hunted animal went to waste. That reverence held for generations.

But as people grew more numerous and more skilled, they took more and gave back less, and the old reverence thinned. Settlers spread far and wide, raising villages of wood and stone (fishing villages, logging villages, smithing villages, trading villages) linked by dirt and stone roads. One trading village in particular began to stand above the rest: Babylonia.

"You know you're alone, but your senses are screaming that you're surrounded by something, watching you just out of view."
The Kingdom

The Kingdom of Babylon

Location
Far north of Silva Aetherea, on a great lake fed by the ocean
Origin
A humble fishing and farming village, Babylonia
Known for
Wealth, innovation, and the study of Magic Stones

Built on the banks of a great lake connected to the ocean by a river, Babylonia was established far in the north of Silva Aetherea. The lake gave fish and fresh water, and the unusually fertile soil produced large yields. It began as a humble fishing and farming village trading with smaller settlements inland, but as it gained wealth it expanded its walls, grew its population, and drew in a concentration of creative and innovative minds.

After the discovery of Magic Stones, Babylonia opened mines throughout the forest and gathered the Stones in great supply. Its scholars studied and experimented with their power, applying them to combat, transportation, construction, and agriculture. With that wealth, the village grew into a kingdom, renamed the Kingdom of Babylon.

A City of White Stone

The wealthiest and largest civilization in Silva Aetherea is also held to be its most beautiful. Tall walls of white limestone and marble, topped with intricate carvings overlaid in metal, ring the kingdom. Beyond the great gates lies a bustling market with stables to either side, and a central fountain fed by a diverted river that runs down the kingdom's spine. Rooftop gardens overflow with flowers, crops, berries, and small trees, and Magic Stone is woven into the infrastructure itself, from the lamps lining the streets to amenities in the homes.

At the northernmost point sits the Royal Palace, a pyramid of smooth white limestone and polished marble pillars, each tier crowned with the rarest flowers spilling over its sides. A colosseum in the northeast hosts scientific demonstrations, plays, performances, and speeches. And from the gates of Babylon all the way to the southern villages runs the Silk Road, a web of paths that begins as wagon-width dirt in the south and widens into cobblestone as it climbs toward the wealthy north.

Power

The Magic Stones

Deep in a mountain mine, a lone miner once struck a wall of stone and saw a blue light shining through the cracks. As he chipped away, the ordinary rock crumbled to reveal a glowing blue stone the size of his palm, cold to the touch and draining: the longer he held it, the more his energy left him. That night, when a fire broke out in his cabin, the stone answered the single word in his mind, water, and loosed a blast that doused the flames. It was the first time in centuries a human had used a Magic Stone.

Elemental Power, at a Price

The Stones can wield the elements (water, stone, wind, fire, ice, even lightning), and rarer Stones still conceal unknown powers. They drive innovation and growth, but they make equally dangerous weapons. They carry two universal drawbacks: they drain the wielder's mana through use (worse against bare skin), and, far more concerning, prolonged contact slowly corrupts the mind, as if the Stone itself has a will encroaching on its bearer's.

The mana drain could never be fully removed, but it could be reduced by channeling a Stone's power through a vessel such as a weapon or tool, which also eliminates the risk of the mind being consumed. This breakthrough, made famous by the minds of Babylonia, even let the Stones power intricate machines once thought impossible. Today the Stones are highly valued: most small villages can afford only one, and nowhere holds a greater supply than the Kingdom of Babylon.

Chapter 1

The Village of Autumn's Rest

Founded
~60 years ago, by the brothers Romeo & Casanova Avhallen
Named for
Their mother, Autumn, who passed crossing the mountain pass
Location
A pass in the Silver's Peaks, between the Grasslands and Silva Aetherea

Autumn's Rest sits on a mountain pass between two peaks of the Silver's Peaks range, surrounded by elm, sugar maple, and pine. The Silver's Peaks separate the southern Grasslands from Silva Aetherea to the north, and no one travels between the two lands without first passing through. For decades the village has served as a rest point for travelers and adventurers making that crossing.

It is neither large nor wealthy. About a dozen families call it home, running the inns, taverns, and shops that keep it afloat. So far south that the Kingdom of Babylon holds little sway, the people rely on the work of their own hands; with no knights stationed here, only two appointed guardsmen and a small outpost protect the village from bandits and wildlife. It's a cozy, tightly knit place where neighbors look out for neighbors, a culture carried up from the Grasslands by its founders.

Protagonist

Rosalie Nehrai

From
Meadowbloom, a farming village in the Grasslands
Raised by
Her mother, Noa-Primrose, a skilled swordswoman
Wields
Her mother's sword

Rosalie grew up in Meadowbloom, a small farming village in the heart of the Grasslands, an only child raised by her mother Noa-Primrose after her father passed when she was seven. Energetic and tomboyish, forever climbing trees and chasing creatures through the tall grass, she was also deeply kindhearted, the cherished "treasure of Meadowbloom" who sought to befriend everyone she met.

Her parents had once been accomplished adventurers who met through an adventurer's guild and traveled all of Silva Aetherea: her mother a skilled swordswoman, her father a master of Magic Stones and ranged abilities. From Noa-Primrose, Rosalie learned the sword and proved a natural; from her father she learned little before his death, told only that it was an accident, a loss that left an incurable pain in her mother.

The story Rosalie never tired of hearing was of a beautiful kingdom deep in the forest, a place of wealth, innovation, and endless possibility named Babylon. When Rosalie was about twenty, her mother fell gravely ill, beyond any medicine. In her final moments, Noa-Primrose urged her daughter to spread her wings and journey to the Kingdom of Babylon in search of "something more." What that something is would be up to Rosalie.

"I am so proud of you. My little girl, my daughter Rosalie. I love you."

And so, a few days after the funeral, Rosalie holstered her mother's sword and set off on her journey.

The Inciting Event

The Rift in the Sky

On a calm, quiet night, a purple light streaks across the clear sky in a long arc above Silva Aetherea and splits it open into a rift. Dark purple clouds and whiskers of light billow from its edges, smothering the moon and stars before rolling back into nothing. The sky returns to normal, but the unease does not.

Rosalie witnesses the display as she nears Autumn's Rest. The Outpost Guard, watching the same sky, can name no Magic Stone capable of an effect on that scale, and wonders aloud whether it came from somewhere "not of this world." It is the omen that turns a quiet night's stay into something far darker, when monsters of unknown origin lay siege to the village after dark.

"The sky split open like it was sliced with a blade."
