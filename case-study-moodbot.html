Music4U (Mood Bot)

Role Technology Lead
Team Group 10 (INF 172)
Tech Stack React, Vite, Figma

Summary

Music4U is an AI-powered web application that delivers personalized music curation based on real-time emotional input. Built to address the high stress levels of university students, the platform acts as an interactive companion that bypasses passive, generalized playlists in favor of active, conversation-driven emotional regulation.

View Live Site

The Problem Space & Gap Analysis

University students experience high levels of stress stemming from academic expectations, financial challenges, and the life transition to adulthood. While students frequently use music as a coping mechanism, finding the exact music to match a specific emotion in the heat of the moment is often difficult. Current solutions in the market and academia fall short in several key areas:

  • Generalized Platforms: Streaming services like Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube Music offer mood-based playlists, but these are generalized and do not account for an individual's unique emotional response to specific genres.
  • Flawed Emotion Recognition: Academic models that utilize facial recognition or EEG signals rely heavily on offline data collected in controlled settings. These systems often struggle to decode neutral facial expressions and are frequently trained on highly limited datasets, making them unreliable for real-world application.
  • Passive Listening: Wellness apps like Calm focus on meditation rather than active, personalized music recommendations based on user-reported stress levels.

The Solution: A Personalized AI Companion

To address these gaps, we developed Music4U, an AI-powered web application that delivers personalized music curation based on real-time emotional input.

  • Conversational Interface: Instead of relying on passive biometric data, the application uses a conversational chatbot where users can directly express their current emotional state.
  • Custom Library Integration: Users can upload their own sample playlists and assign specific stress levels to their tracks, allowing the AI to learn their individual preferences over time.
  • Data Tracking: The app features a calendar interface that tracks past sessions, mapping out the user's emotional state with a color-coded intensity scale.

Technical Implementation & Architecture

As the Technology Lead, I architected and built the application's frontend using React and Vite to ensure a highly responsive experience across both mobile and desktop browsers. To create a welcoming and engaging environment, I developed custom UI components, integrated specialized icon designs, and programmed interactive music note animations.

  • Unified Application: The main app and its companion research website were merged into a single, cohesive web application, allowing the website to serve as a natural entry point.
  • Continuous Deployment: The project was deployed via GitHub Pages using an automated workflow, enabling our team to push continuous updates without disrupting the live user experience.
  • Ethical Guardrails: I implemented a mandatory disclaimer upon entry, ensuring users understand that the application is a supplementary wellness tool and not a replacement for professional mental health support.

User-Centered Research & Iterative Design

Our development process was heavily guided by user research, ensuring the final product directly addressed student needs. We progressed through multiple phases of testing and iteration:

Preliminary Research

  • Conducted an online survey with 26 independent university students.
  • Data revealed that 100% of participants use music to de-stress or calm their minds.
  • Feedback indicated a strong desire for music that perfectly matches their "exact vibes," reinforcing the need for highly personalized curation rather than broad genre playlists.

Mid-Fidelity Testing & UI Pivots

  • Tested our initial Figma prototypes in-person with five users.
  • Users noted that the initial interface felt "too serious" and the chatbot seemed "lifeless."
  • In response, we completely redesigned the UI, adopting softer, more peaceful color palettes (such as pale yellows and greens) to better promote emotional regulation.

High-Fidelity Validation

  • A final round of testing with five new users yielded a 100% task completion rate.
  • Participants specifically praised the feature allowing them to upload and categorize their own music by stress level.

Current Limitations & Future Roadmaps

While the current prototype effectively demonstrates our core concept, there are technical limitations that outline our future development goals:

  • Persistent Backend: The application currently lacks a backend database; therefore, user data, chat logs, and uploaded songs do not carry over between sessions. Future iterations will focus on implementing full user authentication and database storage.
  • API Integration: There is currently no direct integration with platforms like Spotify or Apple Music. Connecting these APIs will eventually allow users to stream music directly within the app and import existing libraries seamlessly.
  • Biometric Data: While our current model thrives on self-reporting, future scope includes integrating passive mood detection through wearable devices to supplement the chatbot's understanding of the user's physical stress markers.
